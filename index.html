ABOUT

CALL FOR PARTICIPATION

ACM Kolkata Chapter appeals to the faculty members in the East and North-East India who are acting as the Supervisors to nominate their students for the ACM Kolkata ’UG Project Contest Award in Computing for 2025.

A team from an Institute shall consist of UG students in the second/third or final year of their studies in CS/IT or in allied streams. Each team needs nominations from the respective Project Supervisor and HoD/Institute Head. Each institute/department can nominate a maximum of 3 teams for the contest.

HOW TO APPLY?

  1. Fill the nomination form online: Google Form. Upload the scanned copy of nomination form [Doc 2] (signed by supervisor & HoD/Director). Then upload the project description [Doc 3].
  2. Registration Fee: ₹500 per team (up to 4 members); ₹100 for each additional member

Important Dates

  • Deadline for Submission of nominations: XYZ 20, 2025
  • Deadline for Submission of final report: XYZ 1, 2025

DETAILS

Procedure for adjudication:

All entries will be evaluated by a Selection Committee from the ACM Kolkata Chapter. The shortlisted teams will be asked to present in front of a panel of juries on May 11, 2025, at the University of Calcutta, Salt Lake campus. Based on the presentation (with optional demo) and documents uploaded, winners will be selected. Winners will receive cash prizes/vouchers and certificates. All participants in the final will receive ACM certificates for participation.

Awards

The top three teams will receive cash prizes of ₹10,000, ₹7,000, and ₹5,000 respectively. All finalists will be awarded certificates and mementos. Special category awards will be announced during the closing ceremony.

— Rules & Regulations —

Welcome to the ENGINUITY THE HARDWARE ARENA! JIS COLLEGE OF ENGINEERING
This event encourages creativity, innovation, and teamwork as participants design and construct models showcasing their technical skills. To ensure a fair and enjoyable experience for everyone, please adhere to the following rules and regulations:

  • Open to all college students. Teams can consist of 4 to 6 members.
  • All the team members should be from the same college.
  • No team leader or member(s) can be in more than one team.
  • All participants must register online before the deadline. Late entries will not be accepted.
  • Participants can use any materials except for pre-made kits or fully assembled models. All materials must be sourced independently.
  • Teams must prepare a brief presentation (maximum 5 minutes) to explain their model’s functionality and design process.
